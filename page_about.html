Bio

I’m a UI/UX & graphic designer in Austin, Texas.

My experience has given me an extensive knowledge of both print and digital design. I’ve got the skills to prepare a perfect, print-ready file, paired with the skills to take my designs straight into HTML & CSS (or SCSS). I live to create (posters, websites, paintings, even bad ceramics, you name it.) When I’m not obsessing over design blogs or web development forums, I’m out camping with the dogs or dragging my wife to yet another “horrible” comic-book movie.

Some Things You Should Probably Know About Me:

I'm a Smarty-Pants

Okay, so I’m not the “I’m better than you, I know everything” kind of person. What I mean to say is, I’ve got a rather broad range of skills. And when I don’t know something, I work my ass off until I’ve mastered it - whether that means learning prepress procedures (check) or how to code semantic HTML & CSS (double-check) on the job.

Adam West inspires me.

Like every designer, I draw upon a number of inspirations from life, art and design. Also like most other designers, I grew up with comics like Batman, Uncanny X-Men and even MAD magazine. What every other designer seems to miss though, is the awesome influence of the classic, Adam West Batman TV show. There was something impactful about seeing the typography do more of the fighting than the actors in that. “BAM!” “POW!”

I color outside the lines.

I went to art school, and I think in terms of solutions, rather than posters or web pages. Of course, I can make both of those things – but if the idea is to promote a brand, is a poster always the best choice? I like to have fun with my work, and I approach design from a slightly different angle. I’ve got a sense of humor, and I think that shows in a lot of my work.

Be Warned: I love bad puns (as you might’ve guessed from my portfolio.)
